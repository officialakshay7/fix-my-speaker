Disclaimer

Last updated: May 1, 2025

This Disclaimer outlines the limitations of Fix My Speaker and the circumstances in which professional repair should be sought rather than relying on our tool.

1. What Fix My Speaker Can and Cannot Do

Fix My Speaker uses acoustic vibration to assist with removing water and debris from phone and device speaker grilles. It is effective for:

  • Water from splashes, rain, and brief water exposure entering the speaker grille
  • Dust, lint, and fine particulate buildup in the speaker mesh
  • General audio quality restoration from debris-related blockage

It is not effective for and is not a substitute for professional repair when dealing with:

  • Physical damage to the speaker diaphragm, coil, or enclosure
  • Corrosion from saltwater, chlorinated water, or other chemical exposure
  • Blown or failed speaker components
  • Internal circuit board water damage extending beyond the speaker area
  • Any hardware malfunction unrelated to water or debris blockage

2. Results Vary

The effectiveness of sound wave speaker cleaning varies by device model, speaker design, severity of exposure, and other factors. We cannot guarantee any specific outcome. If your speaker shows no improvement after five or more full cleaning cycles, we recommend seeking professional device repair.

3. Use at Your Own Risk

While Fix My Speaker is designed to be safe and uses gentle audio frequencies within the normal operating range of phone speakers, you use the Service at your own risk. We are not responsible for any device damage that occurs before, during, or after using the tool. If your device has visible physical damage, severe water exposure, or electrical issues, power it off and seek professional repair before attempting any cleaning.

4. Saltwater and Chemical Exposure

If your device has been exposed to saltwater, pool water, juice, or any liquid other than clean fresh water, there is an elevated risk of corrosion. Sound wave ejection can remove the liquid, but cannot reverse corrosion already in progress. Seek professional repair promptly after any non-freshwater exposure.

5. Professional Repair Recommendation

We actively encourage professional repair for the situations described above. Attempting to repair a severely damaged speaker through any DIY method risks making the situation worse. Authorized service centres have the tools and expertise to properly diagnose and address hardware damage.
