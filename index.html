The Science

How Does Fix My Speaker Work?

Sound is vibration. Fix My Speaker puts that vibration to work - cycling through targeted frequencies to physically eject water and dust out of your speaker.

When you press the eject button on Fix My Speaker, the tool uses your browser's Web Audio API to generate a precise sequence of sound frequencies - typically ranging from 165 Hz to 1000 Hz. These aren't random tones. Each frequency range is chosen because it resonates with the physical dimensions of common phone speaker grills, maximising the vibration energy transferred to water or debris sitting inside - making this speaker cleaner far more effective than basic vibration apps.

The frequencies cycle automatically every 2 seconds throughout the cleaning session. This cycling is important because a single static frequency can only displace water at one resonance point. By sweeping across a range, the water eject process ensures full coverage - targeting both large water droplets near the surface and finer dust particles deeper in the mesh.

In Sound Wave Mode, a continuous tone drives the diaphragm to push fluid outward for maximum water eject efficiency. In Vibration Mode, a pulsed pattern causes rapid start-stop motion that physically shakes loose stubborn debris. Running both modes together gives you the most thorough clean possible, which is why we recommend completing both cycles after any water exposure.

Fix My Speaker runs for 15 seconds per cycle, giving you a live progress indicator so you know exactly where you are. No guesswork, no timers - just press and wait.

Speaker Health

Why Do You Need to Clean Your Phone Speakers from Water and Dust?

Ignoring water and dust in your speaker grill doesn't just affect sound quality - it can permanently damage your device over time.

Most people treat a muffled speaker as a minor inconvenience and wait for it to dry out on its own. The problem is that while surface moisture evaporates, mineral residue from tap or pool water is left behind inside the speaker mesh. This is exactly why a dedicated water eject tool matters - Fix My Speaker physically drives that water out before minerals have time to deposit and corrode the diaphragm or internal wiring.

Dust is a slower but equally serious problem. Your phone speaker grill has dozens of tiny holes, and each one can accumulate lint, skin particles, and fine dust from pockets, bags, and surfaces. As these particles compact, they muffle sound output gradually - most people don't notice until the speaker is performing at a fraction of its original volume. Running a speaker cleaner monthly prevents this slow decline entirely.

There's also a hygiene angle. Bacteria and mold can grow inside damp, dust-filled speaker grills - especially in humid climates. Regular water eject and dust cleaning with Fix My Speaker takes less than a minute and keeps your speaker both performing well and hygienic.

The bottom line: using a speaker cleaner is basic device maintenance. Just as you clean your screen, charge your battery, and update your software, keeping your speaker clear is part of making your phone last longer and sound better every day.

Emergency Guide

What to Do If Your Phone Falls in Water or Dust Enters Inside It?

The first 60 seconds after water exposure matter most. Here's exactly what to do - and what not to do.

1

Get It Out Immediately

Remove your phone from water as quickly as possible. Every additional second increases the depth of penetration, making the water eject process harder and less complete.

2

Power Off Right Away

Turn your phone off immediately. Do not press buttons, do not plug it in to charge. Electric current through wet components causes short circuits that no speaker cleaner can fix afterward.

3

Wipe the Outside

Dry the outside gently with a soft, lint-free cloth. Pat - don't wipe vigorously - to avoid pushing water further into ports or speaker grills before you run Fix My Speaker.

4

Remove Case and SIM Tray

Take off your phone case and eject the SIM tray to allow any trapped water to escape. Leave the SIM tray out while Fix My Speaker runs its water eject cycle.

5

Run Fix My Speaker

Open this speaker cleaner at full volume and run both Sound Wave and Vibration modes 2–3 times. The water eject cycle works best when started as soon as possible after exposure.

6

Air Dry for 24 Hours

Leave your phone in a dry, well-ventilated area for at least 24 hours after running Fix My Speaker. Avoid hair dryers - heat can damage internal components the speaker cleaner can't reach.

⚠️ Never do these after water exposure: Don't charge your phone, don't use a hair dryer, don't put it in rice (rice does not perform a water eject - it only absorbs ambient humidity), and don't shake it vigorously as this can spread water to components that were previously dry.

Myth Busting

Myths and Facts About Fixing Water-Damaged Speakers

Water damage myths cost you time - and your phone. Here's what's true and what isn't when it comes to water eject and speaker recovery.

MYTH

"Putting your phone in rice overnight will fix a water-damaged speaker."

Fact: Rice performs no water eject from inside a speaker grill. It absorbs ambient air moisture at best. The real damage from water - mineral deposits on the diaphragm, corrosion on contacts - is completely unaffected by rice. This myth persists because phones sometimes recover on their own after 24–48 hours regardless of what they're sitting in. Fix My Speaker actively drives water out rather than waiting.

MYTH

"If your phone is water-resistant, you don't need a speaker cleaner."

Fact: Water resistance ratings (IP67, IP68) refer to the phone chassis - not the speaker grills. Water absolutely enters speaker holes on water-resistant phones, and the speaker still sounds muffled after submersion. This is precisely why Apple built a water eject feature into Apple Watch, and why Fix My Speaker exists for every other device.

MYTH

"A hair dryer on low heat is a safe alternative to a water eject tool."

Fact: Even low-heat settings can reach temperatures that damage speaker components, loosen adhesive around screen seals, or degrade the battery. Fix My Speaker completes the water eject process at room temperature with no heat risk whatsoever.

FACT

"Sound frequencies can physically eject water from speaker grills."

True: This is the same principle behind Apple Watch's Water Lock feature. By playing audio at the resonant frequency of the speaker, the diaphragm vibrates rapidly enough to push water droplets outward through the speaker holes. Fix My Speaker applies this water eject technique with cycling frequencies for broader coverage across all speaker grill sizes.

FACT

"Running a speaker cleaner immediately after water exposure gives better results."

True: The sooner you run Fix My Speaker after water exposure, the less time minerals from tap or pool water have to deposit inside the grill. Even a few minutes can make a meaningful difference in how completely the water eject cycle recovers your speaker's sound quality.
