Eject Water from Your Speaker

Whether your phone slipped into the sink, got caught in rain, or took a splash by the pool — water in your speaker grille is a common and deeply frustrating problem. That muffled, underwater sound quality tells you exactly what happened, and the clock is ticking to fix it properly.

Fix My Speaker uses targeted sound wave frequencies to physically vibrate water droplets out of your speaker chamber. It works on the same acoustic principle as the water ejection feature built into Apple Watch — and it runs in any browser on any device, completely free.

How Sound Waves Eject Water from Your Speaker

When Fix My Speaker plays a frequency through your speaker, the diaphragm inside oscillates rapidly at that frequency. This oscillation creates a physical pushing force — strong enough to overcome the surface tension of water droplets clinging to the diaphragm or mesh.

As the diaphragm moves forward and backward in its oscillation cycle, it pushes trapped water outward through the speaker grille. Sound Wave mode cycles through multiple frequencies (165 Hz to 1,000 Hz) to cover different positions in the speaker chamber, while Vibration mode uses pulsed lower frequencies for a secondary dislodging pass.

Step-by-Step Guide to Ejecting Water from Your Speaker

  1. Turn volume to maximum — stronger vibrations mean more effective water ejection.
  2. Tilt the phone so the speaker grille faces downward, letting gravity assist the ejection.
  3. Select Sound Wave mode and press Start. Let the progress ring complete fully.
  4. Switch to Vibration mode and run a second complete cycle.
  5. Repeat 2–3 times for significant water exposure. Wipe around the speaker area with a dry cloth between cycles.
  6. Test your audio by playing music at full volume. You should hear a noticeable improvement.

What to Do Immediately After Getting Your Phone Wet

The first 60 seconds matter most. Follow this sequence for the best chance of full recovery:

  • Power off immediately to prevent short circuits from water reaching live circuits.
  • Remove your case — water collects between the case and body, keeping the speaker damp longer.
  • Pat dry with a lint-free cloth — absorb surface water without pushing more inside.
  • Do not charge it until fully dry — electricity and water in the charging port is dangerous.
  • Run Fix My Speaker to begin ejecting water from the speaker grille immediately.

After Ejecting Water: Finishing the Drying Process

Fix My Speaker handles the speaker-specific water. For residual internal moisture, place your phone in a sealed bag or container with silica gel packets for 12 to 24 hours. Silica gel absorbs ambient moisture far more effectively than rice and is the best follow-up step after sound wave ejection.

Avoid using heat — no hair dryers, no sunlight, no ovens. Heat at the wrong temperatures can damage the battery, soften adhesives, and create steam that worsens internal moisture.
