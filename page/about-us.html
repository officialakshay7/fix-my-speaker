About Fix My Speaker

We built a simple, free tool to solve a frustrating problem - muffled phone speakers after water or dust exposure. No apps. No installs. Just sound waves.

Our Mission

Speaker Problems Shouldn't Require a Repair Shop

We've all been there - your phone takes a splash and suddenly the audio sounds like it's coming from underwater. Or your speaker gets clogged with pocket dust and the volume just isn't what it used to be.

Fix My Speaker was built on a simple belief: most speaker problems caused by water or dust can be solved at home, for free, in under a minute. No technician needed. No app to download. Just open a browser and press a button.

Our mission is to make that solution available to everyone - on any device, anywhere in the world.

Our Story

Born From a Dropped Phone

Fix My Speaker started with a simple, relatable moment - a phone dropped in water and a speaker that never sounded quite right again. After searching online and finding only paid apps or complicated DIY guides, we decided to build something better.

What started as a personal tool quickly grew into something people across the world found useful. Today, Fix My Speaker helps thousands of people every day restore their audio quality without spending a penny.

The Science

How Sound Wave Technology Works

Fix My Speaker uses the Web Audio API built into every modern browser to generate precise sound frequencies. These cause the speaker diaphragm to vibrate rapidly, physically pushing water droplets and loosening dust particles out through the speaker grille.

1

Frequency Generation

The Web Audio API generates sine wave tones at specific frequencies - starting at 165Hz and cycling through up to 350Hz every 2 seconds.

2

Diaphragm Vibration

These tones cause your phone's speaker diaphragm to vibrate at a rate that physically dislodges water molecules and loosens dust buildup inside the mesh.

3

Frequency Cycling

Unlike static tone tools, our algorithm cycles frequencies every 2 seconds to ensure coverage across the full speaker mesh - not just one spot.

4

Vibration Mode

A second pass using broadband noise generates random vibration patterns that catch particles the sine wave mode may have missed.
